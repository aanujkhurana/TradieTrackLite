# TradieTrack Lite — Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 5 June 2026_

TradieTrack Lite ("the App") is built around a local-first promise. This
document explains exactly what data the App stores, where it lives, and
how (or whether) it is shared with anyone.

If you publish the App on the App Store or Google Play, replace the
placeholder URLs below with the real public URLs that point to the
version of this document that ships with the binary. The store
reviewers must be able to visit a live URL.

## 1. What we store on your device

The App stores, on your device only:

- The job records you create (job name, address, customer name,
  customer phone, customer email, status, notes, scheduled time,
  logged time, photos you attach).
- The photos you take or attach. They live in the App's private
  document directory, not in your public photo library unless you
  choose to share a generated PDF report to it.
- A local preferences file (the App's theme preference, a flag
  indicating whether you have seen the first-run onboarding, a flag
  recording your ad-free purchase entitlement, and the last time the
  App checked the purchase receipt locally).
- Local notification identifiers for the reminders you schedule.

These records are never automatically uploaded anywhere. They are not
transmitted to a TradieTrack-controlled server, because TradieTrack
does not operate one for this App.

## 2. What we collect through third-party services

The free version of the App displays ads served by Google AdMob.
AdMob may collect:

- Device identifiers required to deliver and measure ads (advertising
  ID on iOS and Android, subject to your device's permission
  controls).
- Coarse location derived from IP address and device locale.
- Interaction data with the ad (impression, click, dismissal).

On iOS, the App only requests the App Tracking Transparency prompt
once you tap the area that loads an ad. The prompt is described in
plain English. If you decline, AdMob serves contextual ads that do
not use the device's advertising ID.

On Android, the advertising ID is available by default; you can reset
or delete it from your device's settings at any time.

The paid version of the App (one-time purchase to remove ads) does
not load AdMob. The paid version does not request the ATT prompt.

## 3. In-app purchases

The App offers a one-time in-app purchase called "Ad-free". The
purchase is processed by:

- Apple App Store on iOS, governed by Apple's privacy policy and
  refund rules.
- Google Play on Android, governed by Google's privacy policy and
  refund rules.
- RevenueCat, which acts as a thin receipt-validation service so the
  App can restore your purchase on a new device. RevenueCat receives
  the purchase receipt and the App-defined product identifier. It
  does not receive your job records, your photos, or your customer
  details.

TradieTrack does not store your name, address, or payment card. We
never see your payment details.

## 4. Crash reports (optional)

The App can be configured to send anonymous crash reports through
Sentry, a third-party error monitoring service. Crash reports are
off by default. They are only enabled when the build is configured
with a Sentry DSN by the developer, and the report payload does not
include job records, customer details, or photos. If you would like
crash reporting disabled entirely, you can request this in
Settings → Data safety.

## 5. Children

The App is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly
collect information from children. If you believe a child has
created records on the App, deleting the App from the device will
remove those records.

## 6. Your choices and your data

- You can export all of your job records as a JSON backup from
  Settings → Backup at any time. Save the file in a place you trust.
- You can delete individual jobs from the Jobs list, or delete the
  App to remove all records. There is no way for TradieTrack to
  recover records after you delete them, because they are not stored
  on a TradieTrack server.
- You can disable the advertising identifier on your device.
- You can request a refund through Apple or Google according to
  their policies. See the Refund policy in the App listing.

## 7. Changes to this policy

If we change anything material, we will publish a new version of
this document with a fresh "Last updated" date and the App's
Settings screen will surface a one-time notice. We will not silently
expand the data the App collects.
